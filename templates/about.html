About Us

The formation of the group grew out of a meeting Gandalf had with Thorin in Bree which kindled Thorin's interest in recapturing his long lost family inheritance. (Thorin's grandfather had been the King under the Mountain when Smaug came and took it.) Remembering that he had once known an adventurous Hobbit on his travels in the Shire, Gandalf decided to add Bilbo to their company because he knew that stealth and cunning were preferable to force. Gandalf also believed that someone like Bilbo could keep the sometimes prideful and stubborn Dwarves from rash action. The superstitious Dwarves also considered thirteen to be an unlucky number, and as Gandalf had planned to leave on other business, welcomed a fourteenth to fill into their party.

Thorin II Oakenshield

Son of Thráin, son of Thrór, King under the Mountain is a fictional character in J. R. R. Tolkien's 1937 novel The Hobbit. Thorin is the leader of the Company of Dwarves who aim to reclaim the Lonely Mountain from Smaug the dragon. He is the son of Thráin II, grandson of Thrór, and becomes King of Durin's Folk during their exile from Erebor. Thorin's background is further elaborated in Appendix A of Tolkien's 1955 novel The Return of the King.

Filli and Kíli

Fíli and Kíli were brothers, the youngest of the thirteen dwarves who set out on Thorin Oakenshield's quest along with Gandalf and Bilbo Baggins to reclaim the Lonely Mountain from the dragon Smaug. They were also nephews of Thorin, who was the elder brother of their mother Dís. They were both described as having the best eyesight and the keenest hearing of the dwarves. They were also the quickest to spot the Arkenstone in Smaug's hoard.

Óin (Third Age)

Óin was a Dwarf of Durin's Folk, who accompanied Thorin and Company on the Quest of Erebor. He was the brother of Glóin, and cousin of Balin and Dwalin. Óin was the son of Gróin, and the grandson of Farin. He was also the uncle of Gimli, who later became a member of the Fellowship of the Ring.

Glóin

Gandalf

Gandalf, originally named Olórin, was a Maia who served Yavanna and Manwë. He was sent to Middle-earth in the Third Age to combat the threat of Sauron, and became known as the greatest of the Istari. Gandalf played a key role in the War of the Ring alongside his friends Bilbo Baggins, Frodo Baggins, Samwise Gamgee, Peregrin Took, and Meriadoc Brandybuck, and was one of the few beings in Middle-earth who remained entirely incorruptible by the power of the One Ring.
